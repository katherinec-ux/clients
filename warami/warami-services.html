The Meaning

Warami means welcome

Warami is not symbolic branding; it's a way of operating, a respectful beginning to every engagement.

Grounded in Aboriginal language, Warami represents an invitation to connect. A recognition of presence. A respectful beginning. That philosophy shapes every project, every partnership, every outcome.

Warami Services was built to meet a clear need: a trade services partner that delivers with professionalism, structure and credibility, while authentically representing Indigenous ownership and participation. We enter every project with clarity and respect, build trust through action, and deliver outcomes that stand the test of time.

Tom Bowler

Construction Manager · Co-Founder

Tom brings hands-on construction management experience with a strong focus on delivery, coordination and on-site execution. With a background spanning project management across commercial construction environments, he has a practical understanding of multi-trade integration and what it takes to keep complex builds running to standard.

As Construction Manager, Tom leads all on-the-ground delivery for Warami Services — ensuring clear communication, disciplined scheduling and quality outcomes from mobilisation through to handover.

Construction Management Project Coordination Multi-Trade Integration On-Site Delivery Quality Assurance

Guy Carter

Business Development & Asset Solutions · Co-Founder

Guy brings over a decade of experience spanning asset management, building services and commercial project delivery. A licensed electrician by trade, his career has taken him from hands-on electrical work at Sydney Water through to client relationship management at NSW Health and business development leadership at Airmaster, an award-winning building services company.

His experience across government and private sector engagements — including fire, electrical, HVAC&R and infrastructure programs — gives Warami a deep understanding of procurement environments, stakeholder management and contract delivery frameworks. Guy also serves as Vice Chairman of the Warrea Aboriginal Community Association, supporting Indigenous programs in the Hawkesbury Region.
